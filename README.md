# NHANES 2005-2006 Biological Aging & Physical Activity Intensity

**Author:** Nebyu Amaha
**Affiliation:** Mekelle University
**Project Status:** Manuscript in Preparation

## 📖 Overview

This repository contains the complete R analysis pipeline for investigating the association between **physical activity intensity (peak cadence)** and **biological aging** in the U.S. population.

**Primary Research Question:**  
*Does physical activity intensity determine biological aging pathways independently of overall physical activity volume?*

We utilize three distinct, validated biological aging algorithms to ensure robustness:
1. **PhenoAgeAccel** (Mortality-risk based, Levine et al., 2018)
2. **KDM Biological Age** (Regression-based, Klemera & Doubal, 2006)
3. **Homeostatic Dysregulation (HD)** (Mahalanobis distance from healthy homeostasis)

**Key Finding:** Higher peak walking cadence (intensity) is associated with lower biological age acceleration, independent of total daily step count. The relationship is consistent across age groups and is partially non-linear.
